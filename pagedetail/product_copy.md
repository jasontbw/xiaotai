## Product Overview

The **Plastuf Concrete Curing Blanket** is a high-performance insulated tarp engineered for professional construction applications. Built with a closed-cell EPE (Expandable Polyethylene) foam core sandwiched between durable HDPE outer layers, this blanket delivers exceptional thermal insulation for concrete curing projects in all weather conditions.

Our 3.8mm triple-layer closed-cell foam core ensures efficient heat retention, minimizing temperature fluctuations that can cause thermal cracking and premature drying. The HDPE exterior provides superior tear resistance and waterproof protection, while double-stitched edges guarantee long-lasting jobsite durability.

**Why Plastuf?** We understand that proper curing is critical for achieving maximum concrete strength and longevity. Our blankets are engineered to maintain optimal curing temperatures, reducing project timelines while ensuring consistent, high-quality results that meet or exceed industry standards.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q: What R-value does the Plastuf Concrete Curing Blanket provide?
**A**: The 3.8mm EPE foam core delivers an R-value ranging from 0.084 to 0.109 m²·K/W, calculated based on thermal conductivity of 0.035-0.045 W/(m·K). This provides significant thermal resistance for concrete curing. Note that when combined with an air gap under the blanket, effective thermal performance increases substantially.

### Q: Will the blanket recover its thickness after being compressed for shipping?
**A**: Yes, with proper handling. Our 25-30 kg/m³ density EPE foam is engineered for resilience. After opening compressed packaging, allow 24-48 hours at room temperature for natural recovery. Heating to 40-60°C can accelerate the process. Higher density options (30-35 kg/m³) are recommended for export shipping where extended compression periods are expected.

### Q: How many times can I reuse a Plastuf curing blanket?
**A**: With proper care, Plastuf blankets can be reused for 10-15 construction cycles. The HDPE outer layer resists tears and abrasion, while the closed-cell foam maintains insulation properties. Factors affecting lifespan include jobsite conditions, folding technique, and cleaning frequency. We recommend inspecting blankets between uses and repairing any damage promptly.

### Q: Can these blankets be used in freezing temperatures?
**A**: Absolutely. Plastuf blankets are specifically designed for cold-weather concrete curing. They operate reliably from -60°C to +80°C. When temperatures drop below freezing, we recommend combining Plastuf blankets with ground heating cables or hydronic systems for optimal results. The blanket's insulation significantly reduces heating requirements compared to curing without insulation.

### Q: What's the difference between single-layer and triple-layer EPE?
**A**: Our triple-layer construction provides several key advantages: 1) Enhanced structural stability with improved tear resistance, 2) More consistent insulation performance across the blanket, 3) Better resilience after compression and handling. While single-layer blankets work for light-duty applications, triple-layer construction delivers the durability and performance demanded by professional construction sites.

### Q: How do I determine the correct density for my project?
**A**: Choose density based on your climate and expected use: 20-25 kg/m³ for single-season, mild climate projects; 25-30 kg/m³ (our most popular) for general construction with moderate reuse; 30-35 kg/m³ for heavy-duty, cold climate, or multi-year reuse scenarios. For international shipping where blankets will be compressed for extended periods, we recommend the 30-35 kg/m³ density for maximum recovery.

### Q: What standard sizes are available, and do you offer custom dimensions?
**A**: Plastuf offers three standard sizes to accommodate different project requirements: 12ft × 24ft (288 sq ft) for large commercial slabs, 12ft × 20ft (240 sq ft) for medium areas and bridge decks, and 8ft × 24ft (192 sq ft) for roadways, sidewalks, and narrow sections. Custom dimensions can be manufactured to meet specific project requirements with minimum order quantities. Contact our sales team to discuss your custom sizing needs.

### Q: How should I clean and store Plastuf curing blankets between projects?
**A**: Clean blankets by hosing off concrete residue and mud while wet, then allow to dry completely before folding. Avoid using harsh chemicals or solvents that could degrade the HDPE material. Store blankets in a clean, dry area away from direct sunlight and extreme temperatures. Proper folding (not tightly rolled) helps maintain foam resilience. We recommend hanging or loosely stacking blankets rather than compressing them under heavy items during long-term storage.

### Q: Do Plastuf blankets come with grommets or tie-down points?
**A**: Yes, all standard Plastuf Concrete Curing Blankets include heavy-duty rust-resistant grommets spaced approximately every 3 feet along all edges. These reinforced tie-down points allow secure fastening to formwork, stakes, or weights to prevent wind displacement. Grommets are positioned to ensure even tension across the blanket surface for optimal thermal performance.

### Q: Can Plastuf blankets be used in conjunction with concrete heating systems?
**A**: Absolutely — in fact, they are designed to work together. Plastuf blankets significantly increase the efficiency of hydronic heating systems, electric heating cables, and forced air curing methods by trapping heat that would otherwise escape. Typical energy savings range from 40-60% compared to heating without insulation. The blanket's closed-cell structure also prevents moisture from damaging electrical heating elements.

### Q: What is your warranty policy on Plastuf curing blankets?
**A**: Plastuf warrants all curing blankets against manufacturing defects in materials and workmanship for a period of 12 months from the date of purchase. This warranty covers defects such as premature material degradation, faulty grommet installation, and manufacturing seams that fail under normal use. Warranty claims are evaluated on a case-by-case basis, with options including replacement, repair, or credit toward future purchases. Normal wear and tear from jobsite use is not covered under warranty.

### Q: How do Plastuf blankets compare to traditional straw or burlap curing methods?
**A**: Plastuf blankets offer several significant advantages over traditional methods: 1) **Consistent Performance** — Unlike straw, which has variable insulation and can blow away, EPE foam provides uniform R-value across the entire blanket. 2) **Reusability** — Straw is single-use and often becomes contaminated waste, while Plastuf blankets can be reused 10-15 times. 3) **Moisture Control** — The waterproof HDPE layer prevents rain from washing away curing compounds and maintains proper moisture levels. 4) **Cleanup** — No straw debris to remove from cured concrete, resulting in faster job completion and cleaner site conditions.

### Q: Can these blankets be used for purposes other than concrete curing?
**A**: Yes, while designed specifically for concrete curing, Plastuf blankets are versatile enough for other construction applications including: ground thawing insulation, frost protection for excavations, temporary enclosure insulation during winter construction, equipment protection during transport, and moisture barrier for stored materials. Their durable construction and thermal properties make them valuable for any jobsite requiring insulation or weather protection.
